## How My MBA Shapes My FHIR Practice

### Strategic Alignment
FHIR implementations rarely fail for technical reasons — they fail because stakeholders cannot agree on scope, priorities, or funding models. My MBA training equips me to facilitate executive-level conversations, build business cases for interoperability investment, and align technical roadmaps with organizational strategy.

### Stakeholder Communication
I translate complex FHIR and HL7 concepts into clear language for clinical leadership, legal and compliance teams, and C-suite audiences. Effective communication across disciplines is a skill I developed deliberately through graduate business education and have honed in health IT engagements.

### Financial & Operational Analysis
Understanding the financial implications of integration decisions — build vs. buy, total cost of ownership for FHIR server infrastructure, and ROI frameworks for interoperability mandates — allows me to provide guidance that goes beyond technical architecture.

### Change Management
Large-scale data exchange initiatives require organizational change management alongside technical delivery. My business education grounds my approach in change enablement frameworks that help client organizations sustain interoperability gains after go-live.
