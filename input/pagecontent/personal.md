## What Drives Me

I entered health IT because I believe interoperability is foundational to modern healthcare — and that poor data exchange costs lives, not just efficiency. Every Implementation Guide I author and every FHIR API I help deploy is a step toward a healthcare system where the right information reaches the right person at the right time.

This belief has shown up in the projects I have chosen to pursue independently. I built an open-source eCRF builder ([github.com/JunaidUK/ecrf-builder](https://github.com/JunaidUK/ecrf-builder)) after researching manual processes in clinical trial management — if the relevant data already exists in a hospital EHR, populating a trial form should take minutes, not an hour. It is the same pattern I apply to every engagement: start with a clearly defined process problem, research it properly, and build toward a solution that holds up in the real world.

I am a US/UK dual national and have delivered work across both markets throughout my career. I speak conversational Urdu. These experiences have shaped how I approach collaboration — with an appreciation for context, cultural nuance, and the different ways organizations operate across healthcare systems.
